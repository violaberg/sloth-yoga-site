Benefits of yoga for children

It's no secret yoga is highly beneficial for kids as much as it is for adults. Overall yoga offers wide range of benefits for kids, let's have a look at the biggest rewards.

  • Improves self-control

    Self-control is a crucial part of a child's development. Children with good self-control do better at maintaining friendships, making decisions, and respond calmer to stressful situations. Those practising yoga become more patient and calm.

  • Reduces stress and anxiety

    Very often adults think kids have nothing to stress about but that is not entirelly right. Everyday life is becoming more and more stressful for kids and while many things might be silly to adults, it's not so for them. They need de-stress tools as we all! Yoga is a fantastic way for kids to work through their complicated feelings and keep nervous system healthy for years to come.

  • Improves focus and attention span

    Concentration is one of the hardest skills for kids to master! All kids needs freedom to explore but it is equally important to teach them to maintain focus and attention span to learn new things and build relationship with others. Yoga motivates to concentrate on one task at a time which in return teaches to maintain focus for a longer time.

  • Helps with fine and gross motor skills

    We all know how wobbly can be a child, after all they learn everything from beginning, we all started there. Like any other sports, yoga aids kids to train their balance, hand-eye coordination, and other main movements with small muscles.

  • Builds strength and improves flexibility

    Strength and Flexibility probably comes to mind most often when one thinks of physical activity and being consistent at yoga increases flexibility and grows lean muscle that in return keeps kids safe during other activities.

  • Helps with emotion regulation

    The very basis of yoga is to find a balance between the mind and the body. We as humans, and especially kids, go through a big range of emotions every day, yoga helps to regulate different emotions and learn have to stay calm when needed. The American Journal of Occupational Therapy study discovered that daily yoga can help children with autism (ASD) to remain calm and lower levels of aggression, social withdrawal or anxiety. In fact, yoga is very effective during emotional teenage years as kids go through acceptance of change of their body and navigate hormonal changes.

  • Improves overall health

    Children spend more time sitting on daily basis than they ever did before, very often muscles become tense and yoga can certainly help. Stretching itself relieves tense muscles regularly and yoga has many stretching and bending poses. It can help with digestion and find relief in constipation, improve immunity, decrease asthma symptoms, decrease stomach pain for those suffering with IBS and regulate hormones, blood sugar and insulin levels.

  • Builds confidence

    One of the biggest benefits in our opinion is that yoga helps children develop a healthy relationship with their body, which is such an important and healthy mindset for life but aids when going through teenage years as well. Regular yoga practise teaches them to believe in their capabilities as they learn more moves/poses and become proud of their achievements.

  • Refines coordination and body awareness

    Most of the times yoga studios don't have mirrors so one practising can focus on body's present ability and how pose feels rather than how it looks. Regular practise helps to develop strong mind and body.

  • Improves sleep

    Sleep is incredible part of child's developement. It is just as crucial as healthy nutrition and physical activities. Practising yoga daily can help to relax and prepare for a good night sleep. There is no need for a full hour yoga either, just few minutes of breathing to calm the mind and body will make night time routine much easier.
